Signal Evidence

Confirmed means a direct production source supplied the value. Inferred means the agency derived it from source data. Suppressed means the signal is retained for audit but not allowed to drive the decision.

Evidence treatment rule

Confirmed evidence can support a decision. Inferred evidence needs corroboration. Suppressed evidence stays audit-only.

Breadth rule

A recommendation is strongest when price, flow, risk, and narrative evidence agree inside their freshness policy.
